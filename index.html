About

I am a postoctoral researcher in Senory Cognitive Interaction Laboratory (SCI-LAB) curretly working on neural mechanisms of contextual memory separation in humans, when memories are encoded through different sensory channels. I defended my doctoral thesis in October 2020 at the department of Psychology at Stockholm University, Sweden. My previous research focused on auditory processing during concurrent visual tasks with different levels of perceptual load, measured with electroencephalography (EEG). My general interests include cognitive neuroscience, specifically sensory perception, and data analysis.

Researcher & Data Scientist.

I have always enjoyed acquiring new knowledge and I get very excited about new ideas and research. I have experience in various areas of auditory science, starting from being a musician myself, through sound engineering, up to psychological and psychophysiological effects of sound on humans. I also have experience in conducting research and in data analytics. I am trained in experiment design, data collection, data processing, analysis, and visualization, and manuscript preparation.

Publications

I am a co-author on 11 peer-reviewed articles within the areas of cognitive neuroscience, psychology, psychoacoustics, and psychophysiology. I investigated effects of visual information on auditory brain respones, studied the perceived annoyance of wind turbine noise, collaborated in a series of psychoacoustical studies about sound lateralization and precedence effect, and took part in a large clinical project that investigated electrophysiological correlates of different treatments in spider phobic patients. Finally, I also worked together with a group of researchers studying perception of metaphors in different languages. All of that shows that I am able to find collaborations and work well in different groups and on different topics.
